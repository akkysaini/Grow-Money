PARTNERSHIP MODEL

Partnership Model

At Grow Money, we seamlessly integrate the best of both worlds – the robust capabilities of a Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) and the micro-level financial inclusion prowess of a Microfinance Institution (MFI). Our unwavering belief is anchored in the synergy where MFIs provide financial inclusion at the grassroots, complemented and propelled by the infrastructural strength of NBFCs, fostering job creation and wealth generation for the nation.

With over 23 years of unique experience, Grow Money operates at the intersection of NBFC and MFI, navigating and thriving in the diverse landscapes of both markets. Recognized by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) for playing a significant role in the development of sectors like Transport, NBFCs, in conjunction with MFIs, form the backbone of infrastructural growth.

Grow Money stands as one of the select entities that have strategically partnered with both NBFCs and MFIs, leveraging our expertise to drive financial inclusion and economic development.

Documents Required:

To facilitate a seamless process, the following documents are required for loan applications:

  • Loan Application Form
  • KYC Form
  • NBFC Overview Sheet (Excel Sheet Format)
  • PAN card, Address proof (Certified True Copy) of the Company
  • PAN, AADHAR, PASSPORT (PAN Mandatory) of all Directors
  • RBI Certificate, COI, MOA, and AOA (Certified True Copy)
  • Latest 3 Year Audited Financial Statement and Latest Provisional (Quarterly, Half Yearly) (Certified True Copy)
  • Latest 3 Year ITR with Computation (Certified True Copy)
  • List of Directors and Shareholders on Letter Head of Borrower
  • Net Worth Certificate (Director Cum Promoter)
  • Form DIR 12 with ROC Receipt (Certified True Copy)
  • Copy of Grading Report from Credit Rating Agency
  • Latest Six Months Bank Statement of All Bank Accounts (Soft Copy Required)
  • DIR 3 KYC of All Directors, GST Certificate Copy
  • Returns and certificates filed with RBI during the last three financial years
  • CIBIL Reporting Made During Last Six Months along with Acknowledgment
  • Bifurcation of Secured, Unsecured, and Borrowings from Promoters
